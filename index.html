The methodology · 5 stages

Five layers of analysis, one synthesised brief.

Every stage is independent and inspectable. The synthesis at the end requires convergence between at least two layers — neural data + audience simulation + expert panel — before recommending any creative change.

01

Neural inference

The video runs through TRIBE v2 — a foundation model from Meta AI's brain-imaging research, trained on real fMRI BOLD recordings of people watching naturalistic stimuli (Algonauts2025, Lahner2024, Lebel2023, Wen2017). Output: predicted activation envelopes for 12 brain regions across the runtime — auditory, visual, language, memory, reward, decision-making, social cognition, emotion, default-mode.

02

Content classification + literature fingerprint

The ad is classified along 9 axes (paid-ad vs organic, monologue vs dialog, 1st- vs 2nd-person, vulnerable vs aspirational, etc.). For non-paid-ad content, we look up the expected neural firing pattern for that specific category from a peer-reviewed literature library — so the brain prediction is judged against the right baseline, not a paid-ad template.

03

Audience simulation — ICP persona panel (any size)

A panel of demographically-matched simulated viewers reacts to the ad in their own voice. Each persona has a specified life situation, content diet, and skepticism profile. They produce qualitative reactions and structured behavioral predictions: would they skip in 3 seconds? When would they like? What's their friction? Convergent friction across personas is the strongest signal.

04

Expert panel — three specialist agents

Creative Director — craft, hook strength, pacing, parasocial-lock, memorability. Performance Marketer — KPI forecast, funnel quality, A/B priorities. Brand Strategist — swap test, distinctive brand assets, mental availability, brand-build runway. Each agent receives the neural data from stages 1–2 and grounds their critique in it.

05

Synthesis + Quick Wins

Algorithmic creative metrics extract concrete actions: best 6-second clip for paid-spark cut-down, optimal cut length, exact second to place your CTA, brand-recall index, attention sags. Every recommendation in the final brief is triangulated across at least two of the three independent layers (neural / audience / expert).

The science

Grounded in real fMRI brain scans of real people watching real content.

The neural inference layer is built on TRIBE v2 (d'Ascoli et al., Meta AI, 2026), a foundation model trained on hundreds of hours of fMRI BOLD recordings of subjects watching naturalistic video stimuli. It predicts the activation envelope of 12 cortical regions per video.

Neural foundation model

d'Ascoli, Rapin, Benchetrit, Brookes, Begany, Raugel, Banville, King (2026). A foundation model of vision, audition, and language for in-silico neuroscience. Meta AI.

Selected literature anchors
  • Hasson et al. (2008) — temporal receptive windows in cortex.
  • Lerner, Honey, Silbert, Hasson (2011) — narrative listening.
  • Boyatzis, Rochford, Jack (2014) — fMRI of inspirational coaching.
  • Redcay & Schilbach (2019) — second-person neuroscience.
  • Yeshurun et al. (2017) — DMN narrative comprehension.
  • Raichle (2015) — default-mode network dynamics.
What we predict well — and where we don't

Honest about the model's limits.

Some predictions are well-calibrated. Others have known systematic biases. We surface both transparently in every report.

Reliable predictions
  • — Whether viewers will skip in the first 3 seconds (skip-rate inside 95% CI on first ground-truth comparison)
  • — Friction-point diagnosis (specific lines and moments that multiple personas flag independently)
  • — Brand-fit and creative-craft critique grounded in neural firing pattern
  • — Where the brain pattern diverges from the literature baseline for this content category
Known biases — disclosed in every report
  • — Predicted average watch time inflates ~3–4× vs real audiences (personas attend fully; real viewers scroll-half-attentive)
  • — Predicted like-spike timing biases late vs hook-window impulse-likes
  • — Brain attention is not the same thing as platform retention (different mechanisms entirely)
  • — Calibration to your specific audience requires post-launch data we don't yet have
